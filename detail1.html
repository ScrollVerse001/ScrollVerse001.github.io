《煮茶图》

Boiling Tea · Ding Yunpeng, Ming Dynasty

画作背景 | The Background of the Painting

《煮茶图》由明代画家丁云鹏创作，其诞生与明初茶文化的变革密切相关。明太祖朱元璋下令改革贡茶制度，推崇散茶，促使泡茶法逐渐取代唐代以来的煎茶法，成为主流饮茶方式。丁云鹏作为宫廷画师，以唐代"茶仙"卢仝为题材，借古喻今，描绘了晚明文人所崇尚的质朴、清雅的品茗场景，传递出宁静淡泊的精神追求。

Boiling Tea was created by the Ming Dynasty artist Ding Yunpeng against the backdrop of tea culture reforms in the early Ming Dynasty. Emperor Zhu Yuanzhang ordered the reform of the tribute tea system and promoted loose tea, leading to the gradual replacement of the Tang Dynasty method of decocting tea with tea-brewing as the mainstream practice. As a court painter, Ding Yunpeng took Lu Tong, the "Tea Immortal" of the Tang Dynasty, as his subject. By using the past to reflect on the present, he depicted the simple and elegant tea-tasting scenes cherished by late Ming Dynasty, conveying a spirit of tranquility and detachment.

画中，卢仝神情专注，似在静候茶汤，周围陈设简雅，茶具古朴。人物神态生动，设色清劲秀丽，展现了丁云鹏在人物画与文人题材上的高超技艺。画面虽为唐代人物故事，却融入了明代茶具，如紫砂壶等元素，体现了画家对时代变迁中茶文化演变的敏锐观察与艺术融合。

In the painting, Lu Tong's expression focused as if waiting for the tea to brew. The furnishings are simple and tasteful, and the tea utensils appear antique. The figure is vividly rendered, and the colors are clear, fresh, and elegant, showcasing Ding Yunpeng's superb skill in figure painting and literati themes. Although the painting depicts a Tang Dynasty story, it incorporates Ming Dynasty tea utensils, such as the purple clay pot, reflecting the artist's keen observation of the evolution of tea culture across different eras and his ability to blend historical elements artistically.

《煮茶图》不仅是对饮茶方式的记录，更是对茶人精神的礼赞。卢仝一生追求精神富足，主张质朴无华的茶道理念，这与晚明文人在浮躁时世中寻求内心安宁的精神诉求相契合。画家借茶寄意，表达品茶不仅是品味茶汤，更是涵养性情、修身养性的理念。这幅画传递的宁静与专注，成为茶文化深厚底蕴的象征，也为后世茶道的传承与创新奠定了精神基础。

Boiling Tea Painting is not merely a record of tea-drinking customs; it is also a tribute to the spirit of tea culture. Lu Tong pursued spiritual fulfillment throughout his life and advocated a simple, unpretentious approach to the tea ceremony. This ideal resonated with the late Ming Dynasty's search for inner peace amid a restless and turbulent age. Through tea, Ding Yunpeng expressed the idea that tasting tea is not just about its flavor, but also about cultivating one's character and nurturing the soul. The tranquility and focus depicted in the painting embody the profound heritage of tea culture. This work laid a spiritual foundation for the transmission and innovation of tea culture in later generations.
