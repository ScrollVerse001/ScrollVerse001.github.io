《煮茶图》

Boiling Tea

明代画家丁云鹏以唐代“茶仙”卢仝煮茶典故为题材创作，人物神态生动、设色清劲秀丽，展现了明代文人煮泉品茗的幽雅意趣。

Painted by the Ming Dynasty artist Ding Yunpeng based on the theme of the Tang Dynasty "Tea Immortal" Lu Tong boiling tea, this work features vivid character expressions and clear, elegant coloring, showcasing the refined pleasure of Ming Dynasty literati boiling spring water and savoring tea.

《古砖花供图》

Ancient Bricks with Flowers

清代六舟和尚以全形拓技艺将古砖拓为立体器形，再补绘花卉，使斑驳古砖与鲜活草木交融，开创出一种集金石学、绘画与非遗技艺于一体的艺术形式，赋予历史遗物以新的生命与诗意。

In the Qing Dynasty, Monk Liuzhou used the technique of Holographic Rubbing to transfer ancient bricks into three-dimensional vessel shapes, and then supplemented them with painted flowers, blending the mottled ancient bricks with vibrant plants. This created an art form that integrates epigraphy, painting, and intangible cultural heritage techniques, endowing historical relics with new life and poetic charm.

《千里江山图》

A Thousand Miles of Rivers and Mountains

北宋王希孟18岁所作的青绿山水巅峰之作，以矿物颜料绘出千里江山之壮阔，峰峦江河、村落桥梁精细入微。

A pinnacle of blue-green landscape painting created by Wang Ximeng of the Northern Song Dynasty at the age of eighteen, depicting the grandeur of a thousand miles of rivers and mountains with mineral pigments and rendering the peaks, rivers, villages, and bridges with exquisite detail.
